Inside Our 90-Day Digital Marketing Roadmap For New Clients

The exact phases — audit, quick wins, scale — we run through with every new brand we onboard.

EN

Enovexa Solutions — Marketing Team

March 31, 2026 · 6 min read

Every new client asks some version of the same question in week one: "What are you actually going to do, and when will we see results?" Fair question — and one too many agencies answer with vague promises instead of a plan. At Enovexa, every brand we onboard moves through the same three phases over 90 days, in the same order, whether it's a local service business or a funded startup.

We didn't build this timeline to look tidy on a slide. It's the sequence that actually works: you can't scale spend on a broken funnel, and you can't claim quick wins without first knowing what's actually underperforming. Here's exactly how the 90 days break down.

i
Why 90 days, specifically

Thirty days is enough to audit and fix the obvious leaks. Sixty gives quick wins time to compound and produce clean data. Ninety is roughly one full attribution cycle for most paid and organic channels — enough signal to scale spend with confidence instead of guesswork.

01Days 1–30: The Audit Phase

Nothing gets built or spent on until we know exactly what we're working with. The first 30 days are entirely diagnostic — we're mapping the current state of every channel before touching a single campaign:

  • Analytics and tracking audit — verifying GA4, conversion events and pixels are firing correctly. It's shocking how often a client's "underperforming" campaign is actually a tracking gap.
  • Funnel and website review — where visitors drop off between the ad click and the conversion, and why.
  • Competitor and market positioning — who else is bidding for the same customer, and what they're doing better or worse.
  • Channel performance breakdown — which existing spend is actually working, and which is quietly burning budget.

The audit ends with a prioritized findings document, not a wall of raw data — every client gets a ranked list of what's broken, what's working, and what to fix first.

02Days 31–60: Quick Wins

With the audit findings in hand, phase two is about proving momentum fast — fixing the highest-impact, lowest-effort issues before touching long-term strategy:

1. Fixing tracking and attribution gaps

Anything flagged in the audit gets corrected first. Optimizing toward broken data just compounds the problem, so this always comes before any spend changes.

2. Cleaning up underperforming ad sets

We pause or restructure campaigns bleeding budget on the wrong audiences, and reallocate that spend toward whatever the audit showed was already converting.

3. Landing page and funnel fixes

Small, high-leverage changes — clearer calls to action, faster load times, shorter forms — usually lift conversion rate well before any bigger redesign is warranted.

4. Content and creative refresh

Fatigued ad creative and stale on-page content get replaced with variations built around what the audit identified as the actual value proposition working for that audience.

5. SEO foundation fixes

Missing meta data, broken internal links, and technical issues flagged in the audit get resolved so organic growth has a clean base to build on.

"Clients don't need to see everything fixed by day 60 — they need to see the right things trending in the right direction."

— Enovexa Marketing Team

03Days 61–90: Scale

Once tracking is clean and the quick wins have produced real data, phase three shifts from fixing to growing:

Focus Area What We Do
Budget reallocation Shift spend toward the channels and campaigns with proven ROAS from phase two
Audience expansion Build lookalike and retargeting audiences off the converting segments we identified
Creative testing Run structured A/B tests on ad creative and messaging at higher volume
Content scaling Expand SEO and content production around the topics already driving traffic

By day 90, the client has a channel mix backed by real performance data, not assumptions — and a clear view of what to keep scaling into the next quarter.

04What This Looks Like Onboarding-Side

For a new client, the practical experience of these 90 days looks like this:

  • Week 1: Kickoff call, access to accounts, and tracking audit begins
  • Week 2–4: Full audit delivered with prioritized findings and a phase-two plan
  • Week 5–8: Quick wins implemented, weekly progress updates
  • Week 9–12: Scaling plan executed, first full performance review at day 90

🎯 Key Takeaway

  • Every new client goes through the same three phases: audit, quick wins, scale
  • Nothing scales until tracking is verified and the funnel is diagnosed first
  • Quick wins in days 31–60 build the data needed to scale spend with confidence
  • By day 90, decisions are backed by real performance data, not guesswork

The roadmap doesn't end at day 90 — it just moves into an ongoing cycle of testing and scaling from a much stronger baseline. If you're evaluating a marketing partner and they can't tell you specifically what happens in week one versus week nine, that's worth asking about before you sign anything.
